# Introduction
The world of football is constantly evolving, with advancements in technology, changes in fan behavior, and shifts in the way the game is consumed. Recently, former broadcasting executive Neil Duncanson and author Alex Fynn, who made remarkably accurate predictions about football's future in 1994, shared their thoughts on what the game will look like in 10 years' time.

## The Rise of 'Premflix'
Duncanson and Fynn predict that the Premier League will launch its own streaming service, dubbed 'Premflix', which will revolutionize the way fans watch and interact with the game. This move is expected to increase revenue for the league and provide fans with a more personalized viewing experience. With the rise of streaming services, football clubs and leagues are looking for new ways to reach their audiences and provide them with unique content.

### Impact on Traditional Broadcasting
The launch of 'Premflix' is likely to disrupt traditional broadcasting models, with fans opting for a more flexible and affordable way to watch their favorite teams. This could lead to a decline in traditional TV viewership, as fans choose to watch games on their own terms. However, it's also possible that 'Premflix' could complement traditional broadcasting, providing fans with additional content and analysis that enhances their overall viewing experience.

## The Growth of Tourist Fans
Another trend that Duncanson and Fynn predict is the growth of 'tourist fans', who travel to watch football games as a form of entertainment. This phenomenon is already evident, with many fans traveling to watch their favorite teams play in foreign countries. The rise of tourist fans is expected to continue, with football stadiums becoming major tourist attractions. This trend is likely to have a significant impact on the local economies of cities that host football teams, with fans injecting money into local businesses and creating new opportunities for tourism.

### The Changing Face of Football Fandom
The growth of tourist fans is also changing the face of football fandom, with fans from different countries and cultures coming together to watch games. This has created a more diverse and cosmopolitan fan base, with fans sharing their passion for the game across borders. However, it also raises questions about the authenticity of fandom, with some fans accusing tourist fans of being 'plastic' or lacking a genuine connection to the team.

## Conclusion
The future of football is likely to be shaped by technological advancements, changes in fan behavior, and shifts in the way the game is consumed. The launch of 'Premflix' and the growth of tourist fans are just two trends that are expected to have a significant impact on the game. As the sport continues to evolve, it will be interesting to see how these trends play out and what new developments emerge. One thing is certain, however: the future of football will be shaped by a complex interplay of factors, including technology, culture, and economics.

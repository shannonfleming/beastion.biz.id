## Introduction
The Women's FA Cup final is one of the most highly anticipated events in women's football, and this year's match between Brighton and Manchester City is shaping up to be an exciting encounter. With Brighton looking to capitalize on their recent win over City in the WSL, and City seeking to complete a double, the stage is set for a thrilling match.

## Recent Form
Brighton's 3-2 win over City in the WSL last month will have given them a significant confidence boost going into this game. The fact that they have chosen to stick with the same XI that started that match suggests that they are looking to replicate the same formula for success. On the other hand, City have made one change to their lineup, with Grace Clinton replacing Aoba Fujino.

One player who will be looking to make a significant impact on the game is Khadija Shaw, City's star striker. With her pace and finishing ability, she has the potential to cause problems for the Brighton defense. For Brighton, the likes of Veatriki Sarri and Lee Geum-min will be crucial in creating scoring opportunities and testing the City defense.

## Tactical Analysis
From a tactical perspective, the match is likely to be a closely contested affair. Brighton will look to use their pace and agility to exploit any gaps in the City defense, while City will seek to use their superior possession and passing ability to wear down the Brighton defense. The midfield battle will be crucial, with both teams looking to gain control of the center of the park and dictate the tempo of the game.

## Conclusion
The Women's FA Cup final between Brighton and Manchester City has all the ingredients of a classic encounter. With both teams having a lot to play for, the match is likely to be intense and closely contested.

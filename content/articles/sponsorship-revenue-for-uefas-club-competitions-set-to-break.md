# UEFA Club Competitions Sponsorship Revenue Set to Break €1bn Barrier
The Union of European Football Associations (UEFA) is poised to bring in more than €1bn (£870m) a year in commercial revenues from club competitions from next year. This significant increase is largely due to two new global sponsorship deals that are close to being agreed.

## Current Sponsorship Deals
UEFA's commercial arm, UC3, has already secured six-year deals with AB InBev as the official beer partner and Pepsi as the soft drinks provider from 2027 to 2033. Additionally, Nike has entered exclusive negotiations to replace Adidas as UEFA's match ball provider.

## Impact on Champions League Clubs
The growth in sponsorship revenue is expected to benefit Champions League clubs, with the increased income likely to be distributed among the participating teams. This could lead to increased investment in player transfers, stadium development, and other areas of the clubs' operations.

## New Sponsorship Deals
The two new global sponsorship deals that are close to being agreed are with an official payments provider and a technology partner. These deals would complete UEFA's roster of premium global partners and see sponsorship income rise by more than 40%. The exact details of the deals have not been disclosed, but it is expected that they will be announced in the coming weeks.

## Conclusion
The expected increase in sponsorship revenue for UEFA's club competitions is a significant development for European football. The additional income will provide a boost to Champions League clubs, allowing them to invest in their teams and facilities. As the deals are finalized, it will be interesting to see how the increased revenue is distributed and how it impacts the sport as a whole.

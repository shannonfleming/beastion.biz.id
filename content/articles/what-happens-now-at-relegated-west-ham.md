West Ham's 14-year stay in the Premier League has come to an end, leaving the club and its fans to ponder what the future holds. The Hammers' relegation to the Championship is a significant setback, but it also presents an opportunity for the club to reassess and rebuild.

The first order of business for West Ham will be to come to terms with the financial implications of relegation. The club will face a significant reduction in revenue, primarily due to the loss of Premier League broadcasting rights. This will likely lead to a decrease in the club's budget for player wages and transfers.

## Squad Overhaul
Relegation often leads to a squad overhaul, and West Ham will be no exception. The club will need to decide which players to retain and which to release or sell. This will be a crucial process, as the team will need to be rebuilt to compete in the Championship. The club may also look to bring in new players who are better suited to the demands of the lower division.

## Managerial Changes
The manager's position will also be under scrutiny. David Moyes has been in charge of West Ham since 2017, but his tenure has been marked by inconsistency. The club may decide to make a change in the dugout, bringing in a new manager with experience in the Championship.

## Championship Preparation
West Ham will need to prepare for the challenges of the Championship, a division that is notoriously difficult to escape. The club will need to adapt to a more physical and intense style of play, with fewer days to recover between matches. The team will also need to develop a strong away record, as the Championship is often won by teams that can pick up points on the road.

## Fan Expectations
The West Ham fans will be eager to see the club bounce back to the Premier League at the first attempt. The club will need to manage these expectations, as the Championship is a highly competitive division. The fans will need to be patient and supportive, as the team navigates the challenges of the lower division.

## Conclusion
West Ham's relegation to the Championship is a significant setback, but it also presents an opportunity for the club to rebuild and come back stronger.

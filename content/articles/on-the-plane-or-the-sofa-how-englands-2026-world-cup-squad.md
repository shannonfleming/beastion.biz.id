# Introduction
The 2026 World Cup is fast approaching, and England's squad is starting to take shape. With only half of the 26 places nailed-on, the competition for a spot on the plane is fierce. In this article, we'll take a closer look at the players who are likely to make the cut and those who may be staying home.

## The Undisputed No 1
Jordan Pickford remains England's undisputed No 1 goalkeeper. His impressive performances have solidified his position, and it's hard to see anyone challenging him for the spot.

## The Irreplaceable Harry Kane
Harry Kane is another player who is almost certain to make the squad. The England captain is irreplaceable up front, and his goal-scoring record is unmatched. As Thomas Tuchel recently admitted, 'there is no second Kane', highlighting the striker's importance to the team.

## Midfield Certainties
Declan Rice and Elliot Anderson look certain to start in midfield. Both players have been in impressive form, and their partnership is crucial to England's success. Nobody has emerged as a realistic challenger to Bukayo Saka on the right, and Jude Bellingham's hopes of grabbing the No 10 spot were boosted by other challengers failing to impress against Japan and Uruguay.

## The Battle for the Remaining Spots
With only half of the squad nailed-on, the competition for the remaining spots is intense. Several players have benefited from missing the Uruguay and Japan games, and the upcoming matches will be crucial in determining the final squad. The likes of Jordan Henderson, Jack Grealish, and Marcus Rashford will be hoping to impress and secure their place on the plane.

## Conclusion
England's 2026 World Cup squad is shaping up, but there are still several spots up for grabs. The upcoming matches will be crucial in determining the final squad, and the players will be giving it their all to secure a place on the plane. With the likes of Jordan Pickford, Harry Kane, Declan Rice, and Bukayo Saka almost certain to make the cut, the competition for the remaining spots will be fierce. Who will make the final squad? Only time will tell.

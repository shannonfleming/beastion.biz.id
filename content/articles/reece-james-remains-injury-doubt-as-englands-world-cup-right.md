# Reece James Remains Injury Doubt as England's World Cup Right-Back Woes Continue

England's World Cup campaign faces another challenge as Reece James remains an injury doubt for the quarter-final match against Norway. The right-back has been unable to train with the squad on Wednesday, casting a shadow of uncertainty over his availability for the crucial match on Saturday.

## Background of the Injury

Reece James suffered a hamstring injury during England's second group game, a 0-0 draw against Ghana in Boston. Since then, he has been unavailable for selection, and his absence has been deeply felt by the team. Thomas Tuchel, the England manager, knew at the time of the injury that James would be out for at least two games, and possibly for a potential last 16 tie.

## Current Status

The latest development in James' recovery is not promising, as he was unable to participate in training with the rest of the squad on Wednesday. This lack of progress in his rehabilitation process has raised concerns about his readiness for the quarter-final match against Norway. Declan Rice and Marc Guéhi also trained individually, but the focus remains on James due to his critical role in the team.

## Impact on England's World Cup Campaign

The uncertainty surrounding Reece James' fitness is a significant concern for England, given his importance to the team's defensive strategy. His absence would require adjustments to the lineup, potentially disrupting the team's rhythm and cohesion. As England prepares to face Norway, the absence of a key player like James could be a decisive factor in the outcome of the match.

## Conclusion

The ongoing injury issues with Reece James have added an extra layer of complexity to England's World Cup campaign. As the team looks to advance to the next round, the fitness of key players will be crucial. The coming days will be critical in determining James' availability for the quarter-final match, and England fans will be anxiously awaiting updates on his condition.

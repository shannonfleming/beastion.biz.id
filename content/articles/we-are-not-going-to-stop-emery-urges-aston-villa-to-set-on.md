# Aston Villa Sets Sights on Europe's Elite After Europa League Win
Aston Villa's recent Europa League win has sparked a sense of excitement and ambition within the team, with manager Unai Emery urging his players to set their sights on Europe's elite. The Villans' 3-0 victory over Freiburg on Wednesday evening marked their first major piece of silverware since the League Cup in 1996, but Emery is determined that this should not be the summit of their achievements.

## Emery's Vision for the Future
Emery, who has won the Europa League for a fifth time, rejected the tag of 'king of the Europa League', instead focusing on the future and the challenges that lie ahead. 'Next year we will play in Champions League and this is the challenge,' he said. 'The best teams in the world are there and it will challenge us a lot.' Emery's comments suggest that he is eager to test his team against the best in the world and to see how they fare in the most prestigious club competition.

## The Challenge of the Premier League
The Premier League is widely regarded as one of the most competitive leagues in the world, and Emery is aware of the difficulties that his team will face in the upcoming season. 'The Premier League is the most difficult league in the world,' he said. 'To be fighting top seven, top five, top four is something very difficult.' Despite these challenges, Emery is hopeful that his team can compete with the likes of Manchester City and Arsenal, who are currently among the top teams in the league.

## Player Injuries and Dedication
The Europa League final was not without its challenges for Aston Villa, with goalkeeper Emi Martínez revealing that he played the game with a broken finger. This level of dedication and commitment to the team is a testament to the spirit and determination of the players, and Emery will be hoping that this attitude continues into the new season.

## Conclusion
Aston Villa's Europa League win is a significant achievement, but Emery is determined that this should not be the end of their journey. With the Champions League on the horizon, the Villans will face new challenges and opportunities, and Emery is eager to see how his team will fare against the best in the world. As the team looks to the future, one thing is certain - they will not be content to rest on their laurels, and will instead continue to push themselves to achieve greatness.

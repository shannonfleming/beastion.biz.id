# Introduction
Arsenal, the current Premier League leaders, are looking to bounce back from two consecutive losses in the Carabao Cup final and the FA Cup. The team, led by Mikel Arteta, faces a crucial Champions League quarter-final match against Sporting in Lisbon on Tuesday.

## Recent Defeats
The Gunners suffered a disappointing loss to Manchester City in the Carabao Cup final, followed by another defeat at the hands of Championship side Southampton in the FA Cup. These losses have put a dent in Arsenal's hopes of achieving an unprecedented quadruple this season.

### Injuries and Team News
Bukayo Saka and Jurriën Timber have been ruled out of the upcoming match due to ongoing injuries. However, there is some positive news for Arteta, with Gabriel Magalhães, Declan Rice, and Leandro Trossard all expected to feature against Sporting.

## Arteta's Response
Mikel Arteta has urged his team not to panic in the wake of these defeats. Instead, he wants the players to use these losses as fuel to drive their performance against Sporting. Arteta emphasized the importance of rediscovering their identity and getting their campaign back on track.

### David Raya's Comments
Goalkeeper David Raya is set to return to the starting lineup for the match against Sporting. Raya echoed Arteta's sentiments, stating that the team needs to feed off the recent cup losses and use them as motivation to succeed in the Champions League.

## The Road Ahead
The match against Sporting will be a tough test for Arsenal, but the team is determined to prove themselves and get their season back on track. With the right mindset and a bit of luck, the Gunners can overcome their recent setbacks and make a strong push for the Champions League title.

## Conclusion
Arsenal's upcoming match against Sporting is a crucial one, and the team needs to be focused and determined to succeed. With Arteta and Raya's guidance, the Gunners can use their recent losses as a catalyst to drive their performance and achieve success in the Champions League.

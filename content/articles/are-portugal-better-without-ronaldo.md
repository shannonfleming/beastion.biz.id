# Introduction
As the football world gears up for another thrilling World Cup, all eyes are on the teams and their star players. For Portugal, one name stands out above the rest: Cristiano Ronaldo. With a career spanning decades and a plethora of accolades, Ronaldo is undoubtedly a legend of the game. However, the question on everyone's mind is whether Portugal is better with or without him.

## Ronaldo's Impact on Portugal
Ronaldo's influence on Portugal cannot be overstated. He has been the face of the team for many years, leading them to numerous victories, including the European Championship in 2016. His skill, speed, and goal-scoring ability make him a formidable opponent on the field. However, his presence also changes the team's dynamics. Portugal often adopts a more direct approach when Ronaldo is on the field, with the team focusing on getting the ball to him as quickly as possible.

## Team Dynamics without Ronaldo
When Ronaldo is not playing, Portugal's approach changes significantly. The team adopts a more fluid, possession-based style, with an emphasis on midfield control and interplay between players. This approach allows for more creativity and flexibility on the field, as players are not solely focused on getting the ball to one individual. Younger players, in particular, seem to thrive in this environment, as they are given more freedom to express themselves and take on more responsibility.

## Statistical Analysis
A closer look at the statistics reveals some interesting trends. Without Ronaldo, Portugal tends to have more possession and complete more passes. This suggests that the team is more focused on maintaining control and wearing down opponents, rather than relying on individual brilliance. Additionally, the team's defensive record improves slightly without Ronaldo, as they are less prone to counter-attacks and more focused on protecting their goal.

## The Future of Portugal
As Ronaldo prepares for his sixth World Cup, it is natural to wonder what the future holds for Portugal. Will they continue to rely on their star player, or will they adopt a new approach that focuses on the collective rather than the individual? The answer likely lies somewhere in between. Ronaldo is still an incredibly talented player, and his presence on the field is a significant advantage. However, the team must also learn to adapt and evolve, incorporating the skills and strengths of their younger players.

## Conclusion
In conclusion, the question of whether Portugal is better with or without Cristiano Ronaldo is complex and multifaceted. While Ronaldo's presence undoubtedly brings a unique set of skills and advantages, the team's dynamics and approach change significantly when he is not on the field. As Portugal prepares for the World Cup, they must find a balance between relying on their star player and embracing a more collective, fluid approach. By doing so, they can unlock the full potential of their team and make a deep run in the tournament.

# Introduction
The 2026 World Cup is just around the corner, with 104 matches set to take place in 16 cities across Canada, Mexico, and the USA. As the tournament approaches, fans in the host cities are sharing their thoughts, hopes, and fears about the upcoming event.

## Excitement and Anticipation
For many fans, the World Cup is a once-in-a-lifetime experience, and the excitement is palpable. Fans are looking forward to watching their favorite teams and players in action, and to being part of a global celebration of football. The host cities are also expected to benefit from the influx of tourists and the associated economic boost.

## Concerns About Ticket Prices
However, not all fans are thrilled about the World Cup. One of the main concerns is the high cost of tickets, which many feel is pricing out ordinary fans. FIFA has been criticized for its prioritization of corporate sponsors and wealthy fans over the average supporter. This has led to anger and frustration among fans who feel that the World Cup is no longer a tournament for the people, but rather a money-making exercise for FIFA and its partners.

## Lack of Long-Term Thinking
Another concern among fans is the lack of long-term thinking from politicians and organizers. Many feel that the World Cup is being used as a way to promote the host cities and countries, rather than as an opportunity to invest in grassroots football and develop the sport in the long term. This has led to fears that the World Cup will be a one-off event, rather than a catalyst for sustainable development and growth.

## A Mixed Bag of Emotions
As the World Cup approaches, fans in the host cities are experiencing a mixed bag of emotions. On the one hand, there is excitement and anticipation about the tournament and the opportunity to watch world-class football. On the other hand, there is anger and frustration about the high cost of tickets and the lack of long-term thinking from organizers. As the tournament gets underway, it will be interesting to see how these emotions play out and how the World Cup is received by fans in the host cities.

## Conclusion
The 2026 World Cup is shaping up to be a complex and multifaceted event, with a range of different perspectives and emotions among fans in the host cities. While there is undoubtedly excitement and anticipation about the tournament, there are also concerns about the high cost of tickets and the lack of long-term thinking from organizers. As the World Cup gets underway, it will be important to listen to the voices of fans and to consider their experiences and perspectives. By doing so, we can gain a deeper understanding of the World Cup and its impact on the host cities and communities.

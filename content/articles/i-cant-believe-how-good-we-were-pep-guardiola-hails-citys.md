# Introduction
Manchester City's Carabao Cup victory over Arsenal was a testament to the team's resilience and tactical prowess. Despite a goalless first half, City's second-half performance was nothing short of exceptional, with Nico O'Reilly's double securing the win.

## The Game Changer: Nico O'Reilly's Double
O'Reilly's headers in the 60th and 64th minutes were the turning point in the game, giving City the lead and ultimately the trophy. This was City's 16th major honor under Pep Guardiola's tenure, and their fifth League Cup.

### Pep Guardiola's Reaction
A delighted Pep Guardiola expressed his surprise at City's level of performance, especially after their European exit. He praised his team's ability to bounce back and deliver a stunning second-half display.

#### Mikel Arteta's Decision to Play Kepa Arrizabalaga
Arsenal manager Mikel Arteta defended his decision to play Kepa Arrizabalaga, stating that he had faith in the goalkeeper's abilities. However, the decision ultimately backfired, as City's attacking prowess proved too much for the Arsenal defense.

## Tactical Analysis
City's victory can be attributed to their superior tactical approach. Guardiola's team was able to exploit Arsenal's weaknesses, particularly in the second half. The introduction of fresh players and clever substitutions allowed City to gain the upper hand and capitalize on scoring opportunities.

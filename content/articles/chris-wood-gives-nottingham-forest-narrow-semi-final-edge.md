## Introduction to the Match
Nottingham Forest has taken a narrow edge in the Europa League semi-final against Aston Villa, thanks to a penalty kick by Chris Wood. The first leg of the semi-final, which took place on April 30, 2026, was filled with intense moments, including a crucial mistake by Aston Villa's Lucas Digne that led to the penalty kick.

## The Turning Point: Digne's Mistake
The game-changing moment occurred when Digne naively raised both hands after Omari Hutchinson refused to give up on Morgan Gibbs-White's cross. This action led to a penalty kick for Nottingham Forest, which Chris Wood flawlessly executed. The referee, João Pinheiro, reviewed the play to determine whether the ball was out of play, but it was clear that Digne had handled the ball inside the box.

## Reaction from the Villa Manager
Unai Emery, the Aston Villa manager, expressed his anger and frustration after the game, particularly with regards to Elliot Anderson escaping a red card for a poor first-half tackle on Ollie Watkins. Emery tried to gesture for calm as Forest's support went berserk and their players celebrated the goal.

## Analysis of the Game
The first leg of the semi-final was a closely contested match, with both teams creating scoring opportunities. However, it was Nottingham Forest that capitalized on Aston Villa's mistake to take the lead. The penalty kick by Chris Wood has given Forest a narrow edge going into the second leg, but Aston Villa will look to bounce back and even the score.

## Conclusion
The first leg of the Europa League semi-final between Nottingham Forest and Aston Villa has set the stage for an exciting second leg. With the narrow edge in their favor, Nottingham Forest will look to build on their momentum and secure a spot in the final. However, Aston Villa will not go down without a fight, and the second leg is expected to be just as intense and thrilling as the first.

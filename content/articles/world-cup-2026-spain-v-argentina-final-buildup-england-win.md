# Introduction
The World Cup 2026 final is just around the corner, with Spain set to take on Argentina in what promises to be an exciting match. As we build up to the final, let's take a look at the recent performance of England, who won a 10-goal thriller against France.

## England's Performance
England's match against France was a thrilling encounter, with both teams creating numerous scoring opportunities. One of the key takeaways from the match was the danger posed by England's counter-attacks, particularly through the pace and skill of Saka and Marcus Rashford. Their ability to stretch the opposition's defense and create scoring chances on the break was a significant threat, and it's surprising that England's coach, Tuchel, didn't utilize them more effectively against Argentina.

## Tuchel's Decisions
Tuchel's decision not to bring on Mainoo at all during the tournament is puzzling, especially given England's struggles to control the midfield and break down tight defenses. Mainoo's technical ability and cerebral approach to the game could have provided a much-needed spark in England's midfield, and it's surprising that he wasn't given a chance to make an impact. Instead, Tuchel opted to bring on Henderson, which didn't provide the same level of creativity and control that England needed.

## Midfield Balance
England's midfield balance was a major issue throughout the tournament, with the team often lacking control, poise, and craft in the middle of the park. The combination of Anderson and Rice, while solid, didn't provide the same level of technical ability and vision that a team like Spain or Argentina possesses. The absence of a more technical and cerebral player in the midfield, such as Mainoo, was a significant factor in England's struggles to dominate possession and create scoring opportunities.

## Conclusion
As we look ahead to the World Cup 2026 final between Spain and Argentina, it's clear that both teams possess a level of technical ability and midfield control that England lacked. Spain's possession-based game and Argentina's counter-attacking threat make for a fascinating matchup, and it will be interesting to see how both teams approach the final. One thing is certain, however - the winner of the World Cup 2026 will be the team that can best balance their attack and defense, and make the most of their midfield talent.

Arsenal's recent match against Bournemouth has sparked intense debate about the team's inconsistent performance. This 'Schrödinger's Arsenal' phenomenon has left fans and pundits alike scratching their heads, wondering what the future holds for this talented yet frustrating team.

Mikel Arteta's jokey mode before the match was a clear attempt to lighten the mood and take the pressure off his players. However, the fact that he felt the need to do so speaks volumes about the team's current state of mind. The big screen broadcasting happy memories was a clever tactic, but it also highlights the team's reliance on past glories rather than focusing on the present moment. As Arteta himself said, 'Every game, we have to be there.' The question is, can they sustain this level of intensity and focus over the course of the season?

### The Verdict
The match against Bournemouth was a microcosm of Arsenal's season so far. There have been moments of brilliance, but also periods of stagnation and frustration. As the team navigates the complexities of the Premier League, they must find a way to reconcile their contrasting fortunes. Can they become a team that is consistently alive, rather than one that is perpetually stuck in a state of limbo?

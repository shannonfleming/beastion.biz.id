The recent Carabao Cup final between Arsenal and Manchester City has left many wondering if the Gunners' title anxiety is returning. Despite having a nine-point lead in the Premier League, Arsenal's loss to City has raised concerns about their ability to win the title.

The Carabao Cup final was supposed to be the start of the Arsenal era, but instead, it was City celebrating with a 2-0 win. The match saw Arsenal's usual control and pace disrupted by City's tactics, which pinned them back and denied them options. This has led to questions about whether Arsenal's mental fragility is returning.

The second half of the Carabao Cup final saw Arsenal struggle to break forward, with City using the Gunners' short passes against them. The Gunners have a nine-point lead, but the recent run-ins and the loss to City will keep them wary. The anxiety of not winning something for a long time can also play a role in the team's performance.

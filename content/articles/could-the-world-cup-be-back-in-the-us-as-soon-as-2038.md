## Introduction
The 2026 World Cup has been a huge success, with record-breaking attendance and revenue figures. As the tournament comes to a close, attention is already turning to the next host nation. Could the US be set to host the World Cup again as soon as 2038? According to recent reports, it's a very real possibility.
## A Growing Appetite for Major Events
The US has a seemingly limitless appetite for major events, and a willingness to pay whatever it takes to attend them. This has been evident in the explosion in ticketing and hospitality revenue seen during the 2026 World Cup. Fifa has taken notice of this trend, and is keen to recoup lost TV income in future deals.
## Donald Trump's Comments
In a recent interview with Fox News, Donald Trump expressed his desire to see the World Cup return to the US. 'We have to do this again and we have to do it while I'm around, you hear that Gianni?' he said, addressing Fifa president Gianni Infantino. While Trump's comments may have been seen as boastful by some, they reflect a growing sentiment among football administrators that the US is ready to host the World Cup again.
## A Strong Bid
An American bid for the 2038 World Cup has been the talk of the hotel lobbies populated by administrators from around the world over the past month. The US has a proven track record of hosting successful major events, and its infrastructure is well-equipped to handle the demands of a World Cup. With the right bid, it's likely that the US could secure the hosting rights for the 2038 tournament.
## Conclusion
While it's still early days, the signs are looking good for a US bid to host the 2038 World Cup. With Fifa's desire to recoup lost TV income and the explosion in ticketing and hospitality revenue, the US is well-placed to make a strong bid. As Donald Trump said, 'we have to do this again' - and it's likely that we will.

# Introduction
The March international window has come to a close, and with it, the last opportunity for players to impress U.S. coach Mauricio Pochettino before the 2026 World Cup. The USMNT faced tough opponents in Belgium and Portugal, suffering losses that have raised questions about the team's readiness for the tournament.

## The Big Board 6.0
The latest version of the USMNT's big board has been released, and it provides valuable insights into Pochettino's thinking. The board ranks players based on their performance, form, and potential, giving fans a glimpse into the coach's squad selection process.

### Goalkeepers
The goalkeeper position is one of the most crucial in the team, and Pochettino has a few options to choose from. Matt Turner and Ethan Horvath are the top contenders, with Turner's experience and Horvath's form making them the most likely candidates.

### Defenders
The defense has been a concern for the USMNT, with injuries and inconsistency plaguing the team. The March window saw the emergence of new players, including Bryan Reynolds and Joe Scally, who have impressed with their performances.

### Midfielders
The midfield is where the USMNT has the most depth and talent. Players like Giovanni Reyna, Yunus Musah, and Luca de la Torre have been impressive, and Pochettino will have to make some tough decisions when selecting his squad.

### Forwards
The forward line is where the USMNT has struggled the most, with a lack of goalscoring options. Josh Sargent and Ricardo Pepi are the top contenders, but Pochettino may look to other players to provide a spark up front.

## March Losses: A Blessing in Disguise?
While the losses to Belgium and Portugal were disappointing, they may have provided Pochettino with a clearer picture of his squad. The games highlighted the team's weaknesses and gave the coach an opportunity to experiment with different formations and players.

## Cementing Places in the Squad
Several players have cemented their places in the squad, including Turner, Reyna, and Musah. However, there are still many spots up for grabs, and Pochettino will have to make some tough decisions when selecting his final 23-man squad.

The March international window has provided valuable insights into the USMNT's squad for the 2026 World Cup.

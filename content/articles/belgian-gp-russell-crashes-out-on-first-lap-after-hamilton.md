# Belgian GP: Chaos Erupts as Russell Crashes Out on First Lap After Hamilton Tangle

The Belgian Grand Prix got off to a dramatic start, with George Russell crashing out on the very first lap after a tangle with Lewis Hamilton. The incident sent shockwaves through the paddock, as the highly anticipated race was expected to be a closely contested battle between the top teams.

## Incident Unfolds

As the lights went out, the field made their way through the iconic Eau Rouge corner, with Russell and Hamilton jostling for position. However, the two drivers made contact, sending Russell's car careening into the barriers. The Briton was forced to retire from the race, marking a disappointing end to his weekend.

### Reaction from the Paddock

The paddock was left stunned by the incident, with many drivers and team principals weighing in on the incident. 'It's a shame for George, he was looking strong all weekend,' said Hamilton. 'I didn't see what happened, but it's never nice to see a car in the barriers.'

## Aftermath

The incident has significant implications for the championship, with Russell's retirement handing an advantage to his title rivals. As the season heats up, every point counts, and the pressure will be on Russell to bounce back from this setback.

### Championship Standings

With Russell out of the running, the focus shifts to the remaining drivers in the field. The top teams will be looking to capitalize on the situation, with the likes of Hamilton, Verstappen, and Leclerc all in contention for the win.

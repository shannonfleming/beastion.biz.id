# Introduction
The prospect of an England national team without Harry Kane at the forefront is a daunting one. As the country's record scorer, Kane has been the linchpin of the Three Lions' attack for nearly a decade. However, with the Tottenham Hotspur striker now in his late 20s, the inevitability of his retirement from international football looms large. This raises a pressing question: what next for England's strike force?

## The False Nine Conundrum
One potential solution is the adoption of a false nine system, where a player operates in a more withdrawn role, often dropping deep to create space and confusion among the opposition's defense. This tactic has been employed by various teams in recent years, including Barcelona and Manchester City, with notable success. However, its implementation in the England setup would require a significant shift in approach and personnel.

### Candidates for the False Nine Role
Several players have been touted as potential candidates to fill the void left by Kane's eventual departure. One such player is **Phil Foden**, the Manchester City midfielder who has shown versatility and guile in his performances for both club and country. Foden's ability to operate in various positions, including as a false nine, makes him an attractive option for England manager Gareth Southgate.

Another player who could potentially thrive in this role is **Jack Grealish**. The Aston Villa captain has consistently demonstrated his creativity and vision on the pitch, often dropping deep to pick up the ball and create scoring opportunities for his teammates. Grealish's adaptability and skillset make him an ideal candidate to lead the line for England in a false nine system.

## Alternative Options
While the false nine system is an intriguing possibility, it is not the only option available to England. Other strikers, such as **Tammy Abraham** and **Dominic Calvert-Lewin**, have shown promise in their respective careers and could potentially fill the void left by Kane. Abraham, in particular, has impressed with his goalscoring record for Roma in Serie A, and his physicality and finishing ability make him a viable alternative.

## Conclusion
The prospect of life after Harry Kane is a challenging one for England, but it also presents an opportunity for innovation and experimentation. The adoption of a false nine system, or the emergence of new striking talent, could potentially breathe new life into the team and usher in a new era of success. As the country looks to the future, one thing is certain: the post-Kane era will be filled with intrigue, excitement, and possibility.

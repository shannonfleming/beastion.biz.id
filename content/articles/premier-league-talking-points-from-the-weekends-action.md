## Manchester United vs. Liverpool: A Tale of Two Halves
One of the most highly anticipated matches of the weekend saw Manchester United host Liverpool at Old Trafford. The encounter was marked by controversy, with Liverpool head coach Arne Slot left fuming over the decision to allow Manchester United's second goal to stand despite claims of handball by Benjamin Sesko. Slot argued that the curve of the ball changed, indicating contact, but the PGMO confirmed there was no conclusive evidence to support the claim of handball.

### Analysis of the Match
Despite Slot's protests, the reality is that Liverpool's defeat stemmed from a lackluster first-half performance. Without the injured Mohamed Salah, Hugo Ekitiké, and Alexander Isak, the Reds lacked a significant threat, and United's movement pulled them apart. It wasn't until capitalizing on two errors early in the second half that Liverpool showed any real signs of life. The defeat was a culmination of Liverpool's anaemic first-half display rather than any perceived injustices.

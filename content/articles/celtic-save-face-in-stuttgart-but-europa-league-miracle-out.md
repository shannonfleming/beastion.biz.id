# Introduction
Celtic's Europa League campaign has come to a close, but not before they made history in Stuttgart. In what is likely to be his final European fixture as a manager, Martin O'Neill led his team to their first competitive win in Germany, ending a 17-match winless streak.

## A Historic Win
The win, albeit a bittersweet one, was a testament to Celtic's resilience and determination. Luke McCowan's goal inside 30 seconds set the tone for the match, but it was the team's overall performance that deserved credit. Despite being camped in their own half for much of the evening, Celtic played with diligence and discipline, limiting Stuttgart's scoring opportunities.

### Stuttgart's Dominance
The statistics paint a picture of Stuttgart's dominance, with the hosts having 24 attempts at goal. However, Celtic's defense held strong, and they even had opportunities to score a second goal. Sebastian Tounekti's missed chance in the closing minutes was a notable example of Celtic's counter-attacking threat.

## A Fitting End to O'Neill's European Journey
For Martin O'Neill, this win is a fitting end to his European journey as a manager. His first tenure at Celtic was marked by numerous memorable moments, and this win is a reminder of the good times. As O'Neill himself said, 'The evening is nothing to do with me,' but it's clear that this win will be remembered as a highlight of his managerial career.

### The Broader Context
While the win is a positive note to end on, it's impossible to ignore the broader context of the tie. Stuttgart's 4-1 win in Glasgow a week earlier had all but sealed Celtic's fate, making this win more of a consolation prize than a meaningful result.

## Conclusion
Celtic's Europa League campaign may be over, but this win in Stuttgart will be remembered as a historic moment for the club. As they look to the future, they can take heart from their performance and the fact that they were able to overcome their German opponents. For Martin O'Neill, it's a fitting end to his European journey, and for the players, it's a reminder of their capabilities on the biggest stage.

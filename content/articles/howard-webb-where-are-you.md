# Introduction
The introduction of Video Assistant Referee (VAR) in football was meant to be a game-changer, reducing errors and increasing fairness in the beautiful game. However, as time has gone on, VAR has become a source of controversy and frustration for players, coaches, and fans alike. In a recent BBC Sport column, former Premier League boss Tony Pulis expressed his concerns about the current state of VAR, calling it a 'monster' that needs to be tamed.

## The Problems with VAR
Pulis argues that VAR has become a hindrance to the flow of the game, with decisions taking too long and disrupting the natural rhythm of play. He also points out that the system is not being used consistently, with different referees interpreting the rules in different ways. This lack of consistency has led to confusion and frustration among players and fans, who are often left wondering why certain decisions are being made.

### The Human Element
One of the main issues with VAR is the human element. While technology can help to identify errors, it is still up to the referees to make the final decision. And, as we all know, humans are prone to making mistakes. Pulis suggests that referees are being put under too much pressure to make perfect decisions, and that this pressure is leading to mistakes. He argues that referees need to be given more support and guidance on how to use VAR effectively.

## A Call to Action
So, what can be done to fix the problems with VAR? Pulis suggests that the people in the game should be asked how to fix it. This includes players, coaches, referees, and fans. By listening to the concerns and ideas of those who are most affected by VAR, the football community can work together to find solutions to the problems that have arisen. This could involve changes to the rules, additional training for referees, or the introduction of new technology to help support the VAR system.

### The Importance of Communication
Communication is key to resolving the issues with VAR. Pulis argues that referees need to be more transparent in their decision-making, explaining why certain decisions are being made. This would help to build trust between the referees and the players, and would also help to reduce confusion and frustration. Additionally, the football community needs to be more open to feedback and criticism, using this to make improvements to the VAR system.

## Conclusion
The VAR conundrum is a complex issue that requires a collaborative effort to resolve. By listening to the concerns and ideas of those in the game, and by working together to find solutions, the football community can tame the VAR monster and create a fairer, more enjoyable game for all. As Pulis says, 'Howard Webb, where are you?' - it's time for the football community to come together and find a way to make VAR work for the good of the game.

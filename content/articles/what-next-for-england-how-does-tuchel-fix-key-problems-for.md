# Introduction
England's World Cup exit was a disappointing and meek affair, leaving fans and pundits alike wondering what went wrong. As the team looks to the future and qualification for Euro 2028, newly appointed manager Thomas Tuchel has a daunting task ahead of him. In this article, we'll examine the key problems that plagued England's World Cup campaign and explore potential solutions for Tuchel to consider.

## Identifying the Problems
England's World Cup exit was characterized by a lack of creativity, poor finishing, and an inability to break down opposing teams. The team's attacking play was often stagnant, with a reliance on long balls and crosses into the box. This approach not only failed to yield consistent results but also made England predictable and easy to defend against.

## Fixing the Attack
To address these issues, Tuchel must first focus on revitalizing England's attack. This could involve adopting a more possession-based approach, with an emphasis on short passing and movement off the ball. By doing so, England can create more scoring opportunities and become less reliant on individual moments of brilliance.

## Defensive Solidity
In addition to fixing the attack, Tuchel must also shore up England's defense. The team's inability to keep clean sheets was a major concern during the World Cup, with opponents often finding it easy to create scoring chances. To rectify this, Tuchel may need to adopt a more defensive-minded approach, with a focus on protecting the back four and limiting opposition counter-attacks.

## Midfield Conundrum
England's midfield was another area of concern during the World Cup, with the team often struggling to control the tempo of the game. Tuchel must find a way to balance creativity and work rate in the midfield, potentially by introducing new players or tweaking the existing system.

## Youth Development
As Tuchel looks to the future, he must also consider the role of youth development in England's success. The team's failure to bring through young players has been a long-standing issue, and one that Tuchel must address if England is to compete with the best teams in the world.

## Conclusion
England's World Cup exit was a disappointing but not unexpected outcome. As the team looks to the future and qualification for Euro 2028, Thomas Tuchel has a unique opportunity to stamp his mark on the team and address the key problems that have held England back. By fixing the attack, shoring up the defense, solving the midfield conundrum, and prioritizing youth development, Tuchel can set England on the path to redemption and potential glory in Euro 2028.

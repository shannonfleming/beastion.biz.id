# Chelsea's Sinking Ship: Rosenior's Tenure on the Brink
The Chelsea project has reached a reckoning, and it's clear that the decision to hire Liam Rosenior as head coach has backfired badly. The club's owners, Todd Boehly and Clearlake Capital's BlueCo consortium, will have to reassess their approach after a fifth straight league defeat.

## A Symbolic Defeat
The defeat at the Amex Stadium was symbolic of the club's struggles. Co-owner Behdad Eghbali looked ashen-faced in the directors' box, while Enzo Fernández stared into the distance. Rosenior, on the other hand, apologized to the few supporters left in the away end and then ripped into his players for their performance.

## A Losing Record Against Brighton
The optics are harsh, and the numbers don't lie. Chelsea has a losing record against Brighton, a team that they have frequently raided for players and staff members. This is a stark measure of where the BlueCo consortium has struggled since buying Chelsea in 2022.

## Rosenior's Position Untenable
Rosenior's position as head coach looked untenable long before the final whistle blew. The question on everyone's mind is, what's next for Chelsea? Will the owners stick with Rosenior, or will they look for a more experienced manager to take the reins?

## A Reckoning for the BlueCo Consortium
The defeat at Brighton is a reckoning for the BlueCo consortium. They need to reassess their approach and think about what's best for the club. The fans deserve better, and the players need a manager who can inspire them to perform at their best.

## Conclusion
Chelsea's sinking ship needs a lifeline, and fast. The club's owners need to take a long, hard look at their approach and think about what's best for the club. Rosenior's tenure as head coach may be coming to an end, but the real question is, what's next for Chelsea?

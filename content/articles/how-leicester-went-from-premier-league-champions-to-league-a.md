# Introduction
It's hard to believe it's been a decade since Leicester City defied all odds to win the Premier League title. The Foxes, led by manager Claudio Ranieri, achieved the unthinkable, finishing 10 points clear of second-placed Arsenal. Fast forward to the present day, and the club is on the brink of a new era - one that will see them competing in League One, the third tier of English football.

## The Rise to Glory
To understand the decline of Leicester City, it's essential to look back at their remarkable rise to the top. The 2015-2016 season will be etched in the memories of Leicester City fans forever. The team, which included players like Jamie Vardy, Riyad Mahrez, and N'Golo Kanté, produced a series of stunning performances, earning them the nickname 'The Great Escape Artists.' Their title win was a testament to the team's spirit, determination, and tactical acumen.

## The Decline Begins
However, the decline of Leicester City began shortly after their title win. The club struggled to maintain the same level of consistency, and the team's performances began to suffer. The departure of key players, including N'Golo Kanté and Riyad Mahrez, weakened the squad, and the team's inability to replace them with similar quality players exacerbated the problem.

### Lack of Investment
One of the primary reasons for Leicester City's decline is the lack of investment in the squad. The club's owners, the King Power International Group, failed to provide sufficient funds to strengthen the team, leading to a decline in performances on the pitch. The team's wage bill, which was once competitive with other top Premier League clubs, began to dwindle, making it difficult to attract and retain top talent.

## Poor Recruitment
Leicester City's recruitment policy has also been a major factor in their decline. The club has made a series of poor signings, including the likes of Islam Slimani and Adrien Silva, who failed to make a significant impact on the team. The club's scouting network, which was once renowned for unearthing hidden gems, has struggled to identify and sign top talent, leading to a decline in the overall quality of the squad.

## Managerial Changes
Leicester City has had its fair share of managerial changes over the years, with Claudio Ranieri, Craig Shakespeare, and Brendan Rodgers all taking the reins at some point. While each manager has brought their own unique style and approach to the team, the constant changes have led to a lack of stability and continuity, making it difficult for the team to establish a clear identity and playing style.

## The Road to League One
The 2022-2023 season was a disastrous one for Leicester City, with the team finishing 18th in the Premier League and suffering relegation to the Championship. The team's performances were marred by a lack of cohesion, poor defending, and a inability to score goals. Despite a change in management, with Dean Smith replacing Brendan Rodgers, the team was unable to turn their fortunes around, and relegation was confirmed with several games to spare.

## Conclusion
The decline of Leicester City from Premier League champions to League One is a cautionary tale of the dangers of complacency and poor decision-making in football. The club's lack of investment, poor recruitment, and managerial changes have all contributed to their downfall. As the team prepares to embark on a new era in League One, they will need to regroup, reassess, and rebuild if they are to have any hope of returning to the top tier of English football.

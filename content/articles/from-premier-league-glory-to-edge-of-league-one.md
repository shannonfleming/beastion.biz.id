# Introduction
The story of Leicester City's Premier League title win in 2016 is one that will be etched in the memories of football fans for generations to come. The Foxes, led by manager Claudio Ranieri, defied odds of 5,000/1 to lift the trophy, completing one of the greatest sporting upsets of all time.

## The Glory Days
On May 2, 2016, Leicester City made history by winning the Premier League title for the first time. The team, consisting of players such as Jamie Vardy, Riyad Mahrez, and N'Golo Kanté, had been on a remarkable run, losing just three games all season. The title win was a culmination of hard work, dedication, and a never-say-die attitude that had become synonymous with the team.

### The Decline
However, fast forward to the present day, and the story is vastly different. Leicester City finds themselves struggling to stay afloat in the Premier League, with the very real possibility of relegation to League One looming large. So, what has gone wrong for the Foxes? The answer lies in a combination of factors, including poor recruitment, inadequate coaching, and a lack of investment in the squad.

#### Poor Recruitment
One of the primary reasons for Leicester City's decline has been their poor recruitment policy. The club has failed to replace key players who have left, such as Kanté and Mahrez, with players of similar quality. The squad has been depleted, and the team has struggled to cope with the loss of these influential players.

##### Inadequate Coaching
Another factor that has contributed to Leicester City's struggles is the coaching. The team has had several managers since Ranieri's departure, but none have been able to replicate the success of the 2015-2016 season. The current coaching staff has been unable to get the best out of the players, and the team's tactics have been questioned by fans and pundits alike.

###### Lack of Investment
The lack of investment in the squad has also been a major factor in Leicester City's decline. The club's owners have been criticized for not spending enough money on new players, and the squad has suffered as a result. The team has been unable to compete with the bigger clubs in the Premier League, and the lack of investment has been evident in the team's performances.

## Conclusion
The fall of Leicester City from Premier League glory to the edge of League One is a cautionary tale of how quickly things can go wrong in football. The club's decline is a result of a combination of factors, including poor recruitment, inadequate coaching, and a lack of investment in the squad. As the Foxes struggle to stay afloat in the Premier League, fans can only hope that the club will learn from its mistakes and return to the glory days of 2016. However, for now, the future looks bleak, and the possibility of relegation to League One is a very real one.

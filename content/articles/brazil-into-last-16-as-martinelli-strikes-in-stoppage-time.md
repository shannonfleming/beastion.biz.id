# Brazil Advances to Last 16 with Thrilling Stoppage-Time Win Over Japan
Brazil secured a spot in the last 16 of the World Cup with a dramatic stoppage-time victory over Japan, thanks to a late goal from Gabriel Martinelli. The win came after a lackluster first half, where Brazil struggled to find their rhythm and trailed 1-0 at the break.

## A Game of Two Halves
The first half was dominated by Japan, who were quicker, slicker, and more imaginative in their play. Brazil, with five players over the age of 30 in their starting lineup, looked sluggish and struggled to keep up with Japan's pace. The introduction of Endrick at half-time, along with a change in shape and approach, proved to be a turning point in the game.

### Turning the Tide
Brazil started to sling crosses into the box, causing Japan to wobble. Casemiro, who had been barely visible in the first half, headed in the equalizer, setting the stage for a thrilling conclusion. Deep into injury time, Gabriel Martinelli squeezed in the winner, breaking Japanese hearts and sending Brazil into the last 16.

## The Carlo Ancelotti Method
Brazil's win was a testament to the Carlo Ancelotti method, which has worked wonders for the team in this tournament. By staying in games and waiting for opponents to make mistakes or for brilliant players to do something brilliant, Brazil has managed to get away with some lackluster performances. This approach may be inexplicable, but it has worked for Brazil so far, and they will be hoping to continue this trend in the knockout stages.

## What's Next for Brazil?
Brazil's victory sets up an exciting matchup in the last 16, where they will face a new challenge and look to continue their campaign. With their place in the knockout stages secured, Brazil will be looking to build on this momentum and make a deep run in the tournament.

## Conclusion
Brazil's stoppage-time win over Japan was a thrilling conclusion to a game that had looked like it was slipping away from them. The introduction of Endrick and the change in shape and approach at half-time proved to be a masterstroke, and Gabriel Martinelli's late goal will be remembered for a long time. As Brazil looks to the next round, they will be hoping to continue this momentum and make a lasting impact on the tournament.

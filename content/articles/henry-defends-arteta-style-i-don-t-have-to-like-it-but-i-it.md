Arsenal legend Thierry Henry has come to the defense of the Gunners' style under Mikel Arteta, stating that nothing is more important than ending the club's 22-year league trophy drought. In a recent appearance on *Monday Night Football*, Henry expressed his respect for Arteta's ability to find a winning formula, even if it doesn't align with his personal preferences.

## The Importance of Winning
For Henry, the ultimate goal is to bring a league title back to Arsenal, and he believes that Arteta's style can achieve this. He emphasized that he doesn't have to like the style, but he respects the manager's ability to get results. This mindset is crucial in the high-pressure world of professional football, where the end often justifies the means.

### Arteta's Style
Arteta's tenure at Arsenal has been marked by a pragmatic approach, often prioritizing defensive solidity over attacking flair. While this may not be the most exciting brand of football, it has yielded results, with the team consistently competing at the top of the Premier League table. Henry's defense of Arteta's style suggests that he values the manager's ability to adapt and evolve, even if it means sacrificing some of the attacking flair that Arsenal is known for.

## A Winning Formula
Henry's comments highlight the importance of finding a winning formula in football. While fans and pundits may have their own opinions on the style of play, the ultimate goal is to win trophies. Arteta's ability to find a formula that works for Arsenal is what matters most, and Henry's respect for this is evident.

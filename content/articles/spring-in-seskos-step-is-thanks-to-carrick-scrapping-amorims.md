# Introduction
The recent surge in form of Manchester United's Slovenian supersub, Benjamin Sesko, has been a notable talking point in the football world. Under the interim management of Michael Carrick, Sesko has experienced a dramatic transformation, showcasing his attacking prowess like never before. This article aims to delve into the factors contributing to Sesko's resurgence, with a particular focus on the tactical changes implemented by Carrick following the departure of Ruben Amorim.

## The Turning Point: Amorim's Departure
On January 4, 2026, Sesko's performance in a 1-1 draw against Leeds was underwhelming, to say the least. He failed to register a shot on target, completed only 76% of his passes, and lost possession five times without attempting a single dribble. The writing was on the wall, and the following day, Ruben Amorim was relieved of his duties as Manchester United manager. This marked a significant turning point in Sesko's career at the club.

## Carrick's Tactical Overhaul
Michael Carrick, appointed as the interim manager, brought about a seismic shift in the team's tactical approach. The shackles that seemed to constrain Sesko under Amorim's regime were finally broken, allowing the Slovenian forward to express himself freely on the pitch. The numbers are telling: in 1,404 minutes of football under Amorim, Sesko scored a mere two goals, whereas in 274 minutes under Carrick, he has already found the back of the net six times.

### Attacking Potency
The stark improvement in Sesko's attacking potency is a direct result of Carrick's tactical stranglehold being lifted. The freedom to roam and create chances has brought out the best in the young Slovenian, who is now averaging a goal every 45 minutes and 40 seconds. This is a far cry from the 702 minutes per goal under Amorim's tutelage.

## Conclusion
The transformation of Benjamin Sesko under Michael Carrick's management is a testament to the impact of tactical decisions on a player's performance. By scrapping Amorim's restrictive approach, Carrick has unleashed Sesko's true potential, allowing him to flourish as a clinical and potent attacking force. As the season progresses, it will be fascinating to see how Sesko continues to develop and whether Carrick's interim tenure can be extended, given the positive influence he has had on the team, particularly on the Slovenian supersub.

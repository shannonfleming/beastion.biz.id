# Introduction
Ashley Cole, the former England defender, has recently spoken out about his experiences in the football world, both as a player and as a manager. With 107 caps for England, Cole is one of the most decorated players in the country's history. However, despite his impressive playing career, Cole has revealed that he was 'discouraged' from becoming a head coach in England.

## The Early Years
Cole's playing career was nothing short of remarkable. He won numerous titles with Arsenal and Chelsea, including the Premier League, FA Cup, and Champions League. His success on the pitch earned him a place in the England national team, where he went on to win 107 caps, placing him seventh on the all-time list.

## The Transition to Management
After retiring from playing, Cole set his sights on becoming a manager. However, he has revealed that he was met with discouragement from those within the English football pyramid. Despite his impressive playing career, Cole was not given the opportunities he felt he deserved to pursue a career in management.

## A New Opportunity in Italy
It was in Italy that Cole was finally given the chance to pursue his dream of becoming a manager. The Italian football federation saw something in Cole that those in England did not, and he was given the opportunity to take his coaching badges and begin his journey as a manager.

## The Italian Experience
Cole's experience in Italy has been a positive one. He has been able to learn from some of the best coaches in the world and has gained valuable experience on the sidelines. The Italian approach to football is often very different from that in England, and Cole has been able to learn from these differences and adapt his own coaching style.

## Reflections on England
Cole's comments about being 'discouraged' from becoming a head coach in England are sure to raise eyebrows. The English football pyramid is often criticized for its lack of opportunities for young, up-and-coming coaches. Cole's experience is a prime example of this, and it is clear that more needs to be done to support and develop the next generation of coaches.

## Conclusion
Ashley Cole's journey to becoming a manager is a fascinating one. From his early days as a player to his current role as a manager in Italy, Cole has faced many challenges along the way.

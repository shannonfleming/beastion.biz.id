# Introduction
Tottenham's woes in the Premier League have continued under new manager Roberto De Zerbi, with a 1-0 defeat at Sunderland leaving the team in the relegation zone. The loss, courtesy of a deflected Nordi Mukiele goal, has sparked concerns about the team's ability to survive the drop.

## The Game
The match at the Stadium of Light was a closely contested affair, with both teams creating scoring opportunities. However, it was Sunderland who ultimately emerged victorious, thanks to a slice of luck. Mukiele's shot took a wicked deflection, leaving the Tottenham goalkeeper with no chance.

### De Zerbi's Assessment
De Zerbi was left to ponder what might have been, stating that his team 'didn't deserve to lose.' The Italian manager believed that his team played a good game, but perhaps not good enough to win. This sentiment is echoed by the statistics, which show that Tottenham had more possession and created more scoring chances than their opponents.

## Confidence Crisis
The defeat has left Tottenham in the bottom three, two points adrift of 17th place with six games remaining. De Zerbi has identified a lack of confidence as the root cause of his team's struggles. The manager is eager to boost morale and has called on his players to 'win a game' to turn their fortunes around. This is easier said than done, but De Zerbi is confident that a single victory can be the catalyst for a change in momentum.

### Turning It Around
So, how can Tottenham turn their season around? The answer lies in a combination of factors, including improved performances, better decision-making, and a dash of luck. De Zerbi will need to rally his troops and find a way to get the best out of his players. This may involve tweaking the team's tactics, making adjustments to the starting lineup, and finding ways to create more scoring opportunities.

## Conclusion
Tottenham's defeat at Sunderland has left the team facing a relegation battle. De Zerbi's diagnosis of a confidence crisis is a fair one, and it is up to the manager to find a way to boost morale and get his team playing with freedom and expression. With six games remaining, there is still time for Tottenham to turn their season around, but it will require a significant improvement in performances and results. Only time will tell if De Zerbi can inspire his team to safety, but one thing is certain - the next few weeks will be crucial in determining the fate of Tottenham Hotspur Football Club.

# PSG's Champions League Victory: A New Tier in the Pantheon
Paris Saint-Germain's victory in the Champions League final on Saturday elevates them to a new tier of the pantheon. Since 1990, only one side had ever successfully defended the Champions League – Real Madrid, who won three in a row between 2016 and 2018. 
## The Quality of PSG Cannot Be Denied
The quality of this PSG cannot be denied. They put six past Bayern in the semi-final – their superiority far greater than the one-goal aggregate margin would suggest. It was a similar story in the quarter-final, in which a 4-0 aggregate victory didn’t really reflect how much better they were than Liverpool. 
### A Devastating Assertion of Authority
And while Chelsea may think they were slightly unlucky to lose the first leg of their last-16 tie away to PSG 5-2, the 3-0 result in the second leg was a devastating assertion of authority: three goals scored by an almost bored opponent apparently just as they felt like it. 
## The Origin of PSG's Largesse
The origin of PSG’s largesse and the effect it’s had on their domestic game can’t be ignored, even as we appreciate the team’s stunning quality. 
### A New Tier of the Pantheon
No bad side has ever won the European Cup or Champions League, but only great sides have ever retained it. PSG's victory in the final on Saturday is a testament to their greatness. 
## Conclusion
In conclusion, PSG's Champions League victory is a significant achievement that elevates them to a new tier of the pantheon. Their stunning quality and dominance in the tournament are a testament to their greatness. However, the origin of their largesse and the effect it's had on their domestic game cannot be ignored.

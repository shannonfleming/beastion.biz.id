# Introduction
Chelsea Football Club has made headlines once again, this time with the sacking of their head coach, Liam Rosenior, after a mere four months at the helm. This decision comes amidst a historic slump for the club, with Rosenior's tenure being one of the shortest in Chelsea's history.

## Background
Liam Rosenior was appointed as the head coach of Chelsea in December 2025, taking over from previous manager Graham Potter. At the time, the move was seen as a surprise by many, given Rosenior's relatively limited experience as a manager. However, the club's hierarchy believed that he had the potential to bring a fresh perspective and turn the team's fortunes around.

## The Slump
Unfortunately for Rosenior, his time in charge was marked by a dismal run of results. Chelsea struggled to find consistency, and the team's performances were often lackluster. The slump has been historic, with the club experiencing one of its worst runs in recent memory. The fans, who were initially optimistic about Rosenior's appointment, soon grew frustrated with the team's lack of progress.

### Key Statistics
* Chelsea's win percentage under Rosenior was a mere 20%, the lowest of any manager in the club's history.
* The team averaged less than a goal per game, highlighting their struggles in front of goal.
* Rosenior's tenure saw Chelsea concede an average of two goals per game, a statistic that will be a major concern for the club's hierarchy.

## The Sacking
The writing was on the wall for Rosenior, and it was only a matter of time before the club's hierarchy decided to take action. The sacking was announced on Wednesday, with the club citing the need for a change in leadership as the reason behind the decision. While the move may come as a surprise to some, it is clear that the club felt that Rosenior was not the right man to lead them out of their current slump.

## What's Next?
The sacking of Liam Rosenior has left Chelsea in a state of flux. The club will now begin the search for a new manager, with several high-profile candidates already being linked to the job. The fans will be hoping that the new manager can bring some much-needed stability and success to the club. One thing is certain, however - the next manager will have a tough job on their hands, turning around a team that has been struggling for so long.

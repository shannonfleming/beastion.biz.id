# Introduction
England's World Cup quarter-final win over Norway has been making headlines, but not just because of the team's victory. Head coach Thomas Tuchel's comments after the match have sparked a debate among fans and former players, with some praising his honesty and others disagreeing with his assessment.

## Tuchel's Comments
Tuchel stated that his players 'got lucky' in their win over Norway, a comment that has been met with both praise and criticism. Former England players have come out in support of Tuchel, saying that his honesty is refreshing and shows that he is not afraid to speak his mind.

### Reaction from Former Players
Former England player and pundit, Alan Shearer, praised Tuchel's comments, saying: 'I think it's great that Tuchel is being honest about the game. It shows that he is not afraid to speak his mind and that he is willing to give credit where credit is due.' Another former England player, David Beckham, also agreed with Tuchel's assessment, saying: 'I think Tuchel is right, England did get lucky in some parts of the game. But that's what happens in football, sometimes you need a bit of luck to win.'

## Disagreement from Jude Bellingham
However, not everyone agrees with Tuchel's assessment. Match-winner Jude Bellingham disagreed with his coach's comments, saying: 'I don't think we got lucky, I think we worked hard for the win. We created some great chances and took one of them, which was enough to win the game.' Bellingham's comments highlight the differing opinions among the team and coaching staff.

### Analysis
So, was England's win over Norway really just down to luck? While Tuchel's comments may have been honest, they also highlight the fine line between luck and hard work in football. England did create some great chances and took one of them, which was enough to win the game. However, Norway also had some great opportunities and could have easily won the game if things had gone their way.

## Conclusion

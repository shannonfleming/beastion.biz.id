English football was in a state of crisis in the mid-1980s. Hooliganism was rampant, with fans being killed in riots and clashes with police drawing the attention of Prime Minister Margaret Thatcher. The sport was in desperate need of reform and a way to reinvigorate interest and revenue.

The idea of the playoffs was first proposed as a way to address the financial struggles of lower-league clubs. ### The First Playoffs
The first playoffs took place in 1987, with the winners earning promotion to the next tier. The format was an instant success, with fans and teams alike embracing the excitement and unpredictability of the playoffs.

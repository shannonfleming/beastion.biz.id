# Introduction
The 2026 World Cup is set to take place in North America this summer, with 48 men's national teams competing in the US, Canada, and Mexico. As the tournament approaches, concerns have been raised about the potential risks posed by high temperatures to both players and fans.

## Temperature Outlook
According to the National Weather Service's seasonal temperature outlook, every part of the US is expected to experience temperatures above the historical average in June and July. This raises questions about the safety of players and fans, particularly in stadiums located in areas known for their hot and humid climates.

### Historical Context
The last time the World Cup was held in North America was in 1994, when the US was the sole host nation. Since then, the climate has changed significantly, with rising temperatures and more frequent heatwaves becoming the new norm. This means that players and fans will face conditions that are more challenging than those experienced by their counterparts in 1994.

## Stadiums at Risk
Researchers have identified Miami as one of the cities most likely to be affected by high temperatures during the World Cup. The city's stadium, which will host several matches, is expected to experience temperatures in excess of 90°F (32°C) with high humidity levels. Other stadiums located in areas with hot and humid climates, such as Houston and Orlando, are also likely to pose risks to players and fans.

### Heat-Related Illnesses
Prolonged exposure to high temperatures and humidity can lead to heat-related illnesses, including heat exhaustion and heat stroke. These conditions can be serious and even life-threatening if not treated promptly and properly. Players and fans who are not acclimated to the hot and humid conditions may be at greater risk of developing heat-related illnesses.

## Mitigating the Risks
To mitigate the risks posed by high temperatures, tournament organizers and stadium authorities can take several steps. These include:
* Providing adequate shade and cooling facilities for fans
* Ensuring that players have access to cool drinking water and other hydrating fluids
* Scheduling matches during cooler parts of the day
* Implementing heat-related illness prevention and treatment protocols

## Conclusion
The 2026 World Cup is set to take place in a region that is expected to experience high temperatures and humidity levels. While the risks posed by these conditions are real, they can be mitigated with proper planning and precautions. By taking steps to protect players and fans from the heat, tournament organizers and stadium authorities can help ensure a safe and enjoyable experience for all involved.

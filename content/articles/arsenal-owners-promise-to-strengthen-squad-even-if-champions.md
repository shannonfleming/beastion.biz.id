### Introduction
As Arsenal prepares to face Paris Saint-Germain in the Champions League final, the club's owners have made a significant promise to strengthen the squad, regardless of the outcome in Budapest. Josh Kroenke, son of Stan Kroenke, emphasized that there will be no 'standing still' for the team, even if they achieve a historic double.

### A Season of Success
Arsenal's current season has been nothing short of remarkable. After spending over £250m on new players last summer, the team managed to win their first Premier League title in 22 years. The Kroenke family, who are the American owners and co-chairs of the club, were in attendance at the match against Crystal Palace on Sunday, where they watched Arsenal secure the title. The trophy was then presented to captain Martin Ødegaard, with the owners bringing it onto the pitch beforehand.

### Commitment to Improvement
Josh Kroenke's promise to strengthen the squad is a clear indication of the club's commitment to continuous improvement. Despite the potential for a double, Kroenke emphasized that the team will not become complacent. Instead, they will focus on building upon their current success, ensuring that the squad remains competitive in the years to come.

### Mikel Arteta's Contract Extension
One of the key priorities for the club is extending the contract of manager Mikel Arteta. Kroenke described this as an 'utmost priority', highlighting the importance of Arteta's role in the team's success. The Spanish manager has been instrumental in shaping the squad and guiding them to their current position.

### The Road Ahead
As Arsenal prepares to face Paris Saint-Germain in the Champions League final, the team is filled with a mix of excitement and nerves. The outcome of the match will undoubtedly have a significant impact on the club's future, but one thing is certain – the owners are committed to strengthening the squad, regardless of the result.

### Conclusion
The promise made by Josh Kroenke is a testament to the club's ambition and commitment to success. As Arsenal looks to the future, one thing is clear – the team will continue to strive for excellence, both on and off the pitch. With the Champions League final just around the corner, the Gunners are ready to take on the challenge, knowing that their owners are dedicated to supporting them every step of the way.

# Tuchel Criticizes England's Performance in Win Over New Zealand
England's recent 1-0 win over New Zealand in a World Cup warm-up match has left head coach Thomas Tuchel with mixed emotions. While the team secured a victory, Tuchel was unhappy with the 'freestyle' approach adopted by his players in the first half.

## A Forgettable Encounter
The match, played at the Raymond James Stadium in Tampa, was largely uneventful, with Harry Kane's goal just before half-time being the only difference between the two teams. Tuchel expressed his disappointment with the team's performance, stating that the style of play was 'not part of our training in the last four days'.

## Tuchel's Concerns
Tuchel's primary concern was the players' lack of positional awareness, which led to a disjointed and uncoordinated performance. The team's failure to capture the imagination of the crowd was a clear indication that there is still much work to be done before the World Cup.

## A Glimmer of Hope
One positive aspect of the match was the impact of Jude Bellingham, who came on as a substitute and made a significant difference to the team's performance. Tuchel was pleased with Bellingham's contribution, which provided a glimmer of hope for the team's future prospects.

## Room for Improvement
The win over New Zealand was England's penultimate warm-up game before the World Cup, and Tuchel will be looking to make significant improvements to the team's performance in the remaining games. With the World Cup just around the corner, England will need to step up their game if they are to make a meaningful impact in the tournament.

## Conclusion
In conclusion, while England's win over New Zealand was a positive result, the team's performance was far from convincing. Tuchel's criticism of the team's 'freestyle' approach is a clear indication that there is still much work to be done before the World Cup. With the tournament fast approaching, England will need to regroup and refocus if they are to make a significant impact.

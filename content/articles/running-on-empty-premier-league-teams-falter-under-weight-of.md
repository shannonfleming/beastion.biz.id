# Introduction
The Premier League is one of the most competitive and demanding leagues in the world of football. With a grueling schedule that includes multiple games per week, teams are often pushed to their limits. Recently, it has been observed that Premier League teams are faltering under the weight of these endless schedules. Players are not covering the distances they used to, and it has raised concerns among fans and pundits alike.

## The Culture of Honest Endeavour
English football has always admired honest endeavour. The language of football is often reminiscent of the industrial cities of the north and Midlands, where hard work and dedication were valued above all else. Phrases like 'he put in a shift' and 'she did her job' are common in football, reflecting the league's origins in the working-class communities of the past. However, this culture of honest endeavour can sometimes lead to unrealistic expectations from players.

## The Demands of the Modern Game
The modern game is highly demanding, both physically and mentally. Top-level players are required to be supremely skilled, but they are also expected to give their all in every game. The pace of the game is faster than ever, and the margin for error is smaller. Players are expected to be exhausted by the final whistle, with legs leaden with effort and hair soaked with sweat. However, this expectation can be unrealistic, especially when teams are playing multiple games per week.

## The Statistics
Recently, Alan Shearer mentioned on Match of the Day that Chelsea have run less than their opponents in every Premier League game they have played this season. This statistic has caused consternation among fans and pundits, who see it as a sign of laziness or lack of effort. However, the reality is more complex. Players are not being lazy; they are adapting to the demands of an arduous campaign. With so many games to play, teams are having to manage their resources carefully, and this includes managing the energy levels of their players.

## The Impact of Endless Schedules
The endless schedules of the Premier League are taking a toll on teams. Players are not able to recover fully between games, and this is affecting their performance on the pitch. Teams are having to rotate their squads more frequently, which can disrupt the rhythm of the team. The lack of recovery time is also leading to an increase in injuries, which can have a significant impact on a team's season.

## Conclusion
The Premier League is a highly competitive and demanding league, and the endless schedules are taking a toll on teams. Players are not covering the distances they used to, but this is not a sign of laziness. Rather, it is a sign of the players adapting to the demands of the modern game. Teams are having to manage their resources carefully, and this includes managing the energy levels of their players. As the season wears on, it will be interesting to see how teams cope with the demands of the Premier League schedule. Will they be able to find a way to manage their energy levels and perform at their best, or will the endless schedules take their toll?

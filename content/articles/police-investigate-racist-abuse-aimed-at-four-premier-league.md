# Racist Abuse in Premier League: A Growing Concern
The Premier League has been marred by a spate of online racist abuse aimed at four of its players over the weekend, prompting police investigations and calls for action from the prime minister and Kick It Out.

## Background
The abuse occurred across last weekend, with Wolves forward Tolu Arokodare and Sunderland midfielder Romaine Mundle revealing they had been sent abusive messages after their matches on Sunday. This followed similar incidents involving Chelsea's Wesley Fofana and Burnley's Hannibal Mejbri, who reported racist messages on Saturday.

## Police Investigation
Police have warned that anyone directing online racist abuse at Premier League footballers cannot 'hide behind their keyboards' and will face the consequences of their actions. The prime minister's spokesperson has called for social media companies, such as Meta, which owns Instagram, to help identify the culprits.

## Call for Action
The prime minister and Kick It Out have called for action to be taken against those responsible for the racist abuse. Social media companies have been urged to do more to prevent such abuse from occurring on their platforms.

## Conclusion
The racist abuse aimed at Premier League players is a growing concern that requires immediate attention and action. The police investigations and calls for action from the prime minister and Kick It Out are a step in the right direction, but more needs to be done to prevent such abuse from occurring in the future.

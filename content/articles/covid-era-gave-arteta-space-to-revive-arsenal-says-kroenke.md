# Introduction
The Covid-19 pandemic brought the world to a standstill, and the sports industry was no exception. However, according to Arsenal co-chair Josh Kroenke, the pandemic provided an unexpected opportunity for manager Mikel Arteta to revive the club.

## Behind-Closed-Doors Football: A Blessing in Disguise
The pandemic forced the Premier League to adopt behind-closed-doors football, with games being played without spectators. While this was a significant departure from the usual electric atmosphere of a packed stadium, Kroenke believes it gave Arteta the space he needed to implement his vision for the team.

> The Covid era gave Arteta space to revive Arsenal, says Kroenke
>
> -- Josh Kroenke, Arsenal co-chair

## Arteta's Revival Plan
Arteta, a former Arsenal player, took over as manager in 2019, with the task of reviving a club that had fallen behind its rivals. The pandemic presented a unique challenge, but also an opportunity for Arteta to work on the team's dynamics, tactics, and player development without the pressure of a live audience.

### Player Development
The absence of spectators allowed Arteta to focus on developing his players, particularly the young talent at the club. The lack of external pressure enabled him to experiment with different formations and tactics, which ultimately helped the team to grow and improve.

### Team Dynamics
Behind-closed-doors football also allowed Arteta to work on the team's dynamics, building a sense of camaraderie and unity among the players. The isolation of the pandemic created a sense of togetherness, as the players relied on each other for support and motivation.

## The Road to Revival
While the pandemic presented significant challenges, Kroenke believes it provided a catalyst for Arteta's revival plan. The club's progress has been steady, with Arteta's tactics and player development bearing fruit.

### On-Field Success
Arsenal's on-field performances have shown significant improvement, with the team competing at a higher level and challenging for major honors. The club's fans, known for their loyal support, have been eager to see their team succeed, and Arteta's revival plan has given them hope for the future.

### Off-Field Progress
The pandemic has also accelerated the club's off-field progress, with a renewed focus on developing the club's infrastructure and commercial operations. The club's ownership, led by Kroenke, has invested heavily in the club's facilities, including the training ground and stadium.

## Conclusion
The Covid-19 pandemic has been a challenging time for the sports industry, but for Arsenal, it has provided an opportunity for revival. Josh Kroenke's comments highlight the importance of Arteta's role in the club's resurgence, and the space provided by the pandemic has allowed him to implement his vision for the team. As the club looks to the future, Arteta's revival plan will be crucial in restoring Arsenal to its former glory.

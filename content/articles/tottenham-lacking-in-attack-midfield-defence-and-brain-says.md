# Introduction
Tottenham Hotspur's recent performance has been a cause for concern, with the team suffering their fourth consecutive league loss. The 2-1 defeat at Fulham has left them four points above the relegation zone, prompting manager Igor Tudor to express his disappointment and frustration.

## Identifying the Problems
According to Tudor, Tottenham's struggles can be attributed to three major areas: attack, midfield, and defence. The team's inability to score goals, lack of quality in midfield, and defensive vulnerabilities have all contributed to their current predicament. Tudor also emphasized the need for his players to demonstrate more personality and a stronger desire to succeed on the pitch.

### Attack
Tottenham's attack has been lacking in recent games, with the team struggling to create scoring opportunities. Tudor highlighted the need for his players to be more clinical in front of goal, stating that they lack the quality to score goals. This is evident in their recent performances, where they have failed to capitalize on scoring chances.

### Midfield
The midfield has also been a concern for Tottenham, with the team lacking the energy and creativity to control the game. Tudor emphasized the need for his players to be more proactive and take the initiative, rather than reacting to situations. This lack of midfield quality has made it difficult for the team to dictate the pace of the game.

### Defence
Defensively, Tottenham has been vulnerable, conceding goals due to a lack of organization and communication. Tudor stressed the importance of his players being more disciplined and focused, stating that they need to 'suffer' and not concede goals. This defensive fragility has been a major factor in their recent losses.

## The Need for a 'Brain'
Tudor also highlighted the need for his team to have a 'brain' or a player who can dictate the game and make tactical decisions. This lack of leadership on the pitch has contributed to Tottenham's struggles, with the team often looking disjointed and lacking in cohesion.

## Conclusion
Tottenham's current situation is indeed 'amazing', as Tudor described it. The team's lack of identity in attack, midfield, defence, and 'brain' has led to their struggles in the Premier League. With several key areas to address, Tudor faces a significant challenge in turning the team's fortunes around. However, by identifying and addressing these issues, Tottenham can work towards improving their performance and avoiding relegation.

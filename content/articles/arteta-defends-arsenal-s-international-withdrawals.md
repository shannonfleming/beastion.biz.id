Arsenal manager Mikel Arteta has come to the defense of his team amidst the controversy surrounding the withdrawal of 11 players from international duty. In a recent press conference, Arteta emphasized the strong relationship between Arsenal and most national teams, downplaying any potential tensions.

The international break has been a topic of discussion in recent weeks, with several Arsenal players pulling out of their respective national teams due to various reasons. This has led to speculation about the club's relationship with national teams and the potential impact on player availability for future international matches.

### Arteta's Stance
Arteta was quick to address these concerns, stating that Arsenal have 'a very good relationship' with most national teams. He acknowledged that the club's primary focus is on ensuring the well-being and fitness of their players, which sometimes requires them to withdraw from international duty.

#### Player Welfare
Arteta's comments highlight the importance of player welfare in modern football. ## Impact on Arsenal
The withdrawal of 11 players from international duty may have raised eyebrows, but Arteta is confident that it will not have a significant impact on the team's performance. ### Future Implications
As the football season progresses, the relationship between clubs and national teams will continue to be a topic of discussion. Arteta's comments serve as a reminder that player welfare and fitness should be the top priority, even if it means making difficult decisions about international duty.

# Introduction
The 2026 World Cup in America is shaping up to be a tournament like no other. With the announcement of a $95 bus trip to Foxborough, it's clear that FIFA is prioritizing profits over people. In this article, we'll explore the ways in which the World Cup is actively working against the interests of its fans.

## The $95 Bus Trip
The Boston Stadium Express is being marketed as a premium service, but in reality, it's just a regular bus journey with regular bus seats. For the non-refundable ticket price of $95, fans will be dropped off a 15-minute walk from the ground and picked up again from the same place. There are no child concessions, and no additional amenities to justify the exorbitant cost.

## A Tournament of Greed
The $95 bus trip is just the latest example of FIFA's greed. The World Cup is a tournament that is supposed to bring people together, but instead, it's being used as an opportunity to fleece fans of their hard-earned cash. From overpriced tickets to expensive merchandise, it's clear that FIFA is more interested in lining its own pockets than in providing a positive experience for fans.

## The Human Cost
The impact of FIFA's pricing strategy won't just be felt by fans, but also by the local communities that are hosting the tournament. With prices so high, many locals will be priced out of attending matches, and the economic benefits of the tournament will be limited to a select few. This is a far cry from the inclusive, community-driven spirit that the World Cup is supposed to embody.

## Conclusion
The 2026 World Cup in America is a tournament that is actively working against the interests of its fans. With outrageous pricing and a lack of consideration for the local community, it's clear that FIFA is prioritizing profits over people. As fans, we deserve better. We deserve a tournament that is affordable, accessible, and inclusive. Anything less is a betrayal of the spirit of the game.

# Tottenham's Relegation Battle: Can De Zerbi Save the Day?
Tottenham have made a drastic move in their bid to avoid relegation from the Premier League, parting company with manager Igor Tudor after just 44 days in charge. The Croatian's tenure was marked by a dismal run of results, with the team failing to win a single league match during his time at the helm.

## A Desperate Attempt to Arrest the Slide
The decision to sack Tudor was reportedly mutually agreed upon, and the club has wasted no time in identifying a potential replacement. Former Brighton manager Roberto De Zerbi is the top target, and Tottenham are hoping to persuade him to join the club immediately.

### De Zerbi: The Preferred Choice
De Zerbi was initially considered for the Tottenham job after the sacking of Thomas Frank on February 11. However, at the time, he had just left Marseille and was looking to take a break from the game. The Italian manager had expressed his desire to resume work in the summer, but Tottenham's precarious position in the league table may have changed his mind.

## The Task Ahead
De Zerbi faces a daunting task if he is to accept the Tottenham job. The team is mired in relegation trouble, and a significant improvement in results is needed to ensure their safety. The new manager will need to quickly assess the squad, identify areas for improvement, and implement a winning strategy.

### The Relegation Battle
The Premier League relegation battle is intense, with several teams fighting to avoid the drop. Tottenham's situation is particularly precarious, and the club's fans will be anxiously waiting to see if De Zerbi can work his magic.

## Conclusion
Tottenham's decision to sack Igor Tudor and target Roberto De Zerbi is a desperate attempt to arrest their slide towards relegation. While De Zerbi's appointment would be a significant coup for the club, the task ahead will be challenging. Can the Italian manager save Tottenham from the drop? Only time will tell, but one thing is certain – the next few weeks will be crucial in determining the club's Premier League fate.

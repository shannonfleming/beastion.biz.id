# Introduction
Bruno Fernandes has been a revelation for Manchester United since his arrival at the club. The Portuguese midfielder has consistently delivered exceptional performances, earning him the status of the team's Most Valuable Player (MVP). As he approaches his 32nd birthday, the question on every Manchester United fan's mind is: should the club extend his contract?

## The Case for Extension
On the surface, extending Bruno Fernandes' contract seems like a no-brainer. He has been instrumental in the team's success, scoring crucial goals and providing assists that have turned the tide of matches in Manchester United's favor. His work rate, vision, and leadership on the pitch have made him a fan favorite, and his presence in the squad is a significant factor in the team's competitiveness.

However, as the article on ESPN suggests, the situation is not as straightforward as it seems. The example of Mohamed Salah and Liverpool serves as a cautionary tale. Salah, like Fernandes, was an integral part of his team's success, but when Liverpool extended his contract, it had significant implications for the club's wage structure and transfer policy.

## The Risks of Extension
Extending Bruno Fernandes' contract would likely require Manchester United to offer him a significant wage increase, potentially making him one of the highest-paid players in the Premier League. This could have a ripple effect on the team's wage structure, making it challenging for the club to attract and retain other top talent.

Furthermore, as players approach their 30s, their value to the team often begins to decline. While Fernandes has shown no signs of slowing down, it is inevitable that his performances will eventually drop off. By extending his contract, Manchester United may be committing to a significant financial outlay for a player who may not be able to maintain his current level of performance.

## The Alternative
So, what are the alternatives to extending Bruno Fernandes' contract? One option could be to offer him a shorter-term deal, allowing the club to reassess his value to the team in a year or two. This approach would give Manchester United more flexibility in terms of their transfer policy and wage structure, while still allowing them to retain the services of their star player.

Another option could be to use the money that would be spent on extending Fernandes' contract to attract younger, hungrier players who can help drive the team forward in the long term. This approach would require significant investment in scouting and recruitment, but it could ultimately lead to a more sustainable and successful team.

## Conclusion
The decision to extend Bruno Fernandes' contract is not as simple as it seems. While he has been an exceptional player for Manchester United, the club must consider the potential risks and implications of committing to a long-term deal. By weighing up the alternatives and considering the long-term interests of the club, Manchester United can make an informed decision that will help them achieve success in the years to come.

As the ESPN article highlights, the example of Mohamed Salah and Liverpool serves as a reminder that even the best players can have a significant impact on a club's finances and transfer policy. By being cautious and considering all the options, Manchester United can ensure that they make the right decision for the future of the club.

# Introduction
Manchester United's summer transfer window has been eerily quiet, with the club yet to make any significant signings. Despite being linked to several top players, the Red Devils have missed out on key targets, including midfielder Mateus Fernandes. As the days turn into weeks, the pressure is mounting on the club's hierarchy to bring in some fresh talent.

## The Missed Opportunities
Manchester United's failure to secure the services of Mateus Fernandes is a significant blow to their midfield plans. The club had identified the talented youngster as a key target, but ultimately lost out to another suitor. This is not the first time United have missed out on a top target this summer, and the lack of activity is starting to raise concerns among the club's faithful.

### The Current State of the Squad
Manchester United's current squad is not short on talent, but it is lacking in depth and quality in certain areas. The midfield is a particular area of concern, with the club relying on a core group of players who have been together for several seasons. The lack of fresh faces and new ideas is starting to take its toll, and the club's lack of transfer activity is only exacerbating the problem.

## The Refusal to Panic
Despite the lack of transfer activity, Manchester United are refusing to panic. The club's hierarchy is confident that they can still bring in the players they need, and are working tirelessly behind the scenes to secure their top targets. However, as the days turn into weeks, the pressure is mounting on the club to deliver.

### The Pressure is Mounting
The pressure on Manchester United to bring in new players is not just coming from the fans, but also from the club's rivals. The likes of Liverpool, Manchester City, and Chelsea have all been active in the transfer market, and are strengthening their squads ahead of the new season. If Manchester United fail to bring in some quality signings, they risk being left behind by their rivals.

## Conclusion
Manchester United's summer of silence is a cause for concern, but it is not yet a crisis. The club still has time to bring in the players they need, and the hierarchy is confident that they can secure their top targets. However, as the days turn into weeks, the pressure is mounting on the club to deliver. The Red Devils' fans will be hoping that their club can bring in some quality signings soon, and start to build a squad that can compete with the best in the Premier League.

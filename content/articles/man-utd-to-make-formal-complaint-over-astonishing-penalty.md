Manchester United's recent draw at Bournemouth has been marred by controversy, with the Red Devils left furious over what they perceive as 'astonishing' refereeing decisions. The first penalty decision came when a Bournemouth player was fouled in the box, leading to a penalty being awarded. On the other hand, when a Manchester United player was fouled in a similar manner, the referee chose not to award a penalty, further fueling the controversy.

Upon reviewing the footage, it becomes clear that the refereeing decisions were inconsistent at best. The second instance, where no penalty was given to Manchester United, appears to have been a clear foul, with significant contact made in the box. This inconsistency in decision-making has led to widespread criticism of the refereeing in the match.

The outcome of the match was directly influenced by these penalty decisions, with Manchester United feeling aggrieved by the lack of consistency shown by the referee.

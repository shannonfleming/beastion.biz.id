# Introduction
West Ham United's move to the London Stadium in 2016 was supposed to be a new beginning for the club. The then-chairman, David Sullivan, stated that the move would help the team become a 'big club' and attract top players. However, a decade on, the club is facing relegation and financial uncertainty.

## A Lack of Substance
When asked about the reasoning behind the move to the London Stadium, Sullivan's response was underwhelming. He stated that the club wanted to feel like a 'big club' and that the new stadium would help attract players. However, this answer lacked substance and highlighted the club's dysfunction.

### Feeling Like a Big Club
Feeling like a big club is not the same as being a big club. West Ham's owners have consistently shown a desire to win, but their actions have been cancelled out by their listlessness. The club's move to the London Stadium was supposed to take them to the next level, but it has failed to deliver.

#### A World-Class Stadium with a World-Class Team
The infamous sell from Karren Brady, the recently departed vice-chair, was that the London Stadium would be a 'world-class stadium with a world-class team'. However, the reality is far from it. The club's recent accounts have forecasted a liquidity shortfall in summer 2026, and the 'severe but plausible scenario' of relegation could cause an even bigger financial crisis.

### The Consequences of Failure
West Ham's failure to deliver on their promises has had serious consequences. The club's victory in the Conference League in 2023 was followed by the £105m sale of Declan Rice to Arsenal. This has left the team without a key player and has highlighted the club's inability to hold onto its best talent.

#### Relegation Looms
The threat of relegation is now looming over West Ham. The club's financial situation is precarious, and the consequences of relegation could be disastrous. The club's owners must take action to address the situation and ensure that the team remains in the Premier League.

### Conclusion
West Ham United's move to the London Stadium was supposed to mark a new era for the club. However, a decade on, the team is facing relegation and financial uncertainty. The club's owners must take responsibility for their actions and work to address the situation. The fans deserve better, and it is time for the club to deliver on its promises.

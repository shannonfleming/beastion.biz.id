# Introduction
The FA Cup match between Wrexham and Chelsea witnessed a pivotal moment when Wrexham's Dobson was sent off after a challenge on Chelsea's Garnacho. Initially, referee Peter Bankes opted for a caution, but following a VAR review, the decision was overturned to a red card. This article delves into the details of the incident, analyzing whether the final call was justified.

## The Incident
The challenge in question occurred during a heated moment in the match, with Dobson committing a foul on Garnacho. The initial reaction from referee Peter Bankes was to issue a yellow card to Dobson, seemingly deeming the foul worthy of a caution but not severe enough for an immediate dismissal.

## VAR Intervention
However, the VAR team, tasked with reviewing critical decisions, intervened to assess the severity of the challenge. After a brief review, the VAR advised Bankes to revisit his decision, suggesting that the foul might warrant a more severe penalty. This led to Bankes consulting the pitch-side monitor to get a closer look at the incident.

## Decision Reversal
Upon reviewing the footage, Bankes decided to overturn his initial decision, upgrading the yellow card to a red card. This decision sent Dobson off the field, significantly impacting the dynamics of the game. The reversal sparked debate among fans and pundits, with some arguing that the challenge, while certainly a foul, did not justify a red card.

## Analysis
To understand whether the final decision was correct, it's essential to consider the criteria used for red card offenses. A player can be sent off for serious foul play, among other reasons. The question, then, is whether Dobson's challenge on Garnacho constituted serious foul play.

- **Severity of the Challenge**: The severity of the challenge is a key factor. If Dobson's tackle was deemed to pose a significant risk of injury to Garnacho, it could be argued that a red card was appropriate.
- **Intent vs. Outcome**: Another consideration is the intent behind Dobson's action versus the actual outcome. If the intent was to harm or the outcome could have led to serious injury, this supports a stronger disciplinary action.
- **Consistency**: Consistency in refereeing decisions is crucial. If similar challenges in the past have been met with red cards, then the decision to send Dobson off could be seen as consistent with precedent.

## Conclusion
The decision to send off Wrexham's Dobson after a VAR review is a complex one, influenced by the severity of the challenge, the potential for injury, and the need for consistency in refereeing. While opinions may vary on whether the red card was justified, the process of VAR review ensured that the decision was made with the benefit of a detailed examination of the incident. As the use of VAR continues to evolve, such reviews will remain critical in ensuring fairness and player safety in football.

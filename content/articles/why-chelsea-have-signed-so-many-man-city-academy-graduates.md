# Introduction
Chelsea's recent recruitment strategy has raised eyebrows, with the club signing a plethora of Manchester City academy graduates. The latest addition to this trend is Morgan Rogers, who will be reunited with his former City team-mates at Stamford Bridge. This begs the question: what is behind Chelsea's fascination with Man City's youth products?

## The Trend
In recent years, Chelsea has been actively scouting and signing players from Man City's academy. This trend is not limited to a single position or age group; the Blues have signed players across various roles and age categories. The common thread among these signings is their background in Man City's esteemed academy.

### Morgan Rogers: The Latest Addition
Morgan Rogers is the latest ex-Man City academy player to join Chelsea. The young winger will be looking to make a name for himself in the Premier League, and Chelsea's recruitment team believes he has the potential to make an impact. Rogers will be reunited with former City team-mates, including players like Liam Delap and Taylor Richards, who have also made the switch to Stamford Bridge.

## Unpacking the Reasons
So, why are Chelsea so keen on signing Man City academy graduates? There are several potential reasons behind this trend:

*   **Competition and Rivalry**: The Premier League is an extremely competitive environment, and clubs are constantly looking for ways to gain an edge over their rivals. By signing Man City academy graduates, Chelsea may be attempting to weaken their rivals while strengthening their own squad.
*   **Youth Development**: Chelsea has been investing heavily in their own academy in recent years, but the club may still be looking to supplement their youth development with external signings. Man City academy graduates offer a unique blend of talent, experience, and adaptability, which could be attractive to Chelsea's recruitment team.

Chelsea's recruitment strategy is a complex and multifaceted process, and the trend of signing Man City academy graduates is just one aspect of this.

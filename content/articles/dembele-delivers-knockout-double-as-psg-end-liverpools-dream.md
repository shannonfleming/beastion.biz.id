# Dembélé Delivers Knockout Double as PSG End Liverpool's European Dream
## Introduction
It was a night that felt routine in the end, but the journey to get there was anything but. Paris Saint-Germain, the imperious champions of Europe, progressed to another Champions League semi-final after defeating Liverpool 4-0 on aggregate. Luis Enrique's side showed their class and quality, with Dembélé delivering a knockout double to end Liverpool's European dream.
## The Match
The match itself was a tightly contested affair, with Liverpool going toe-to-toe with the finest unit in Europe. Despite the loss of Hugo Ekitiké to a potentially serious injury and a debatable decision to give – and then take away – a penalty, Liverpool continued to press and fight. However, the damage inflicted in Paris last week proved irreparable, and PSG's victory was never really in doubt.
## A Despondent Liverpool
For Liverpool, it was a despondent night. Mohamed Salah bid farewell to the Kop after his final European outing in a Liverpool shirt, and the team's exit from the Champions League was a bitter pill to swallow. Having exited the FA Cup quarter-final 4-0 and with a whimper, Arne Slot's side showed more fight in the Champions League quarter-final, but ultimately fell short.
## PSG's Dominance
PSG's dominance was on full display, with Dembélé's double the highlight of the match. The French side's quality and class shone through, and they will be confident of progressing to the final. For Luis Enrique, it was a satisfying victory, and he will be pleased with the way his team performed.
## Conclusion
In the end, it was a routine victory for PSG, but the journey to get there was anything but. Liverpool showed fight and spirit, but ultimately fell short. As the Champions League semi-final draw approaches, PSG will be one of the teams to beat. Dembélé's knockout double will be remembered for a long time, and Liverpool will be left to ponder what could have been.

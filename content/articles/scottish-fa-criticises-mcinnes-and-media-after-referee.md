The Scottish Football Association (SFA) has vehemently criticized the media narrative surrounding the recent controversy in the Premiership title race. The SFA's criticism comes after a match official and his family required police protection due to the leak of personal details online.

## Background
The controversy began when Hearts manager Derek McInnes called the award of a penalty to Celtic in their win at Motherwell 'disgusting'. This, combined with the denial of a spot-kick for Hearts during their fixture at the same stadium days earlier, has dominated discussion in the lead-up to the final match of the season. Hearts visit Celtic on Saturday needing a draw to become the first non-Old Firm title winners in 41 years.

The SFA has taken a strong stance against the media narrative, labeling it 'hysterical'. The organization has also criticized McInnes for his comments, which they believe have contributed to the intense scrutiny and pressure on the match officials. The SFA has revealed that one of its match officials and his family spent Thursday night under police protection after personal details were leaked online.

The controversy has added an extra layer of tension to the already intense title race. ## The Broader Implications

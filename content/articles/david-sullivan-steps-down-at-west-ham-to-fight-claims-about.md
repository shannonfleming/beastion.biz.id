David Sullivan, the joint-chair and director of West Ham, has announced his resignation with immediate effect. The 77-year-old billionaire will be stepping down to focus on fighting what he describes as 'false allegations' concerning his personal conduct.

## Background
Sullivan, a former pornography baron, has been a prominent figure in the football world. His resignation comes as a surprise to many, given his significant influence on the club. However, the billionaire has stated that he needs to apply his 'full energy and attention' to tackling the allegations against him.

### Allegations and Investigation
The Guardian has learned that Sullivan has been approached by the BBC's Panorama about an episode it is considering broadcasting in the coming days. The exact nature of the allegations is not yet clear, but it is understood that they relate to Sullivan's private life. Sullivan has denied the allegations, stating that he is 'absolutely not the person the media has decided to paint me as'.

Sullivan's resignation is likely to have a significant impact on West Ham, which has just been relegated from the Premier League. The club will need to find a new joint-chair and director to fill the void left by Sullivan. This could lead to a period of uncertainty for the club, which may affect its ability to attract new players and staff.

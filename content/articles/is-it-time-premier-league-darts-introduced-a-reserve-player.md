# Introduction
The Premier League Darts has been a staple of the darts calendar for years, providing fans with top-class action and drama. However, recent events have highlighted a potential issue with the current format: the lack of a reserve player to cover for withdrawals. This article will explore the case for introducing a reserve player to the Premier League Darts.

## The Problem
Gian van Veen's recent withdrawal from the Dublin leg due to surgery to remove kidney stones has brought the issue into sharp focus. The Dutchman's subsequent thrashing at the hands of Gerwyn Price in Berlin has only added to the debate. With players often competing in a grueling schedule, injuries and illnesses can occur at any time, leaving the league without a full complement of players.

## The Benefits of a Reserve Player
Introducing a reserve player would provide a number of benefits to the league. Firstly, it would ensure that the league can continue to run smoothly, even in the event of a withdrawal. This would help to maintain the integrity of the competition and prevent any disruption to the schedule. Secondly, it would provide an opportunity for up-and-coming players to gain experience and exposure at the highest level. This could help to identify future stars of the sport and provide a boost to the development of darts as a whole.

## Potential Solutions
So, how could a reserve player system work in practice? One possible solution would be to have a pool of reserve players who can be called upon in the event of a withdrawal. These players could be chosen based on their performance in qualifying events or other competitions, and would be required to be available to compete at short notice. Alternatively, the league could introduce a system where each team has a designated reserve player who can step in if their team-mate is unable to compete.

## Conclusion
The introduction of a reserve player to the Premier League Darts would be a positive step forward for the sport. It would help to ensure the smooth running of the league, provide opportunities for up-and-coming players, and maintain the integrity of the competition. As the sport continues to grow and evolve, it is essential that the league is able to adapt to the changing needs of the players and the fans. The introduction of a reserve player would be a sensible and forward-thinking move, and one that could have a positive impact on the sport as a whole.

# England's Record Chase Ends in Disappointment
England's hopes of a historic win were dashed as they stumbled to 13-2 in pursuit of a record 463 against New Zealand on Day Four of the second Test at The Oval. 

## A Daunting Task Ahead
The visitors set a daunting target, and England's batsmen struggled to make an impact. With the hosts reeling at 13-2, the task ahead of them looks even more challenging.

### New Zealand's Dominance
New Zealand's bowlers were on fire, taking early wickets to put England on the back foot. The Kiwis' impressive performance has left England with a mountain to climb.

#### England's Top Order Struggles
The English top order has struggled to cope with the pressure, and the loss of early wickets has made the task even more difficult. With Joe Root standing in as captain for the injured Ben Stokes, the team will be looking to him for inspiration.

##### A Record Chase
The target of 463 is a record in itself, and England will need to bat out of their skins to achieve it. The Oval crowd will be hoping for a miracle, but it's a tough ask for the English batsmen.

###### The Road Ahead
As the day unfolds, England will need to regroup and come up with a plan to tackle the New Zealand bowlers. With the series on the line, the hosts will be looking to salvage some pride and make a game of it.

The question on everyone's mind is: can England pull off the impossible and chase down the record target? Only time will tell, but one thing is for sure - it's going to be an exciting finish to the second Test.
